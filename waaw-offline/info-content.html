The Oracle Has Spoken
Lucila Mayol

Departing from the idea of chance and mysticism conveyed in the Bibliomancy practice, from which Lucila takes the rules and aleatory elements to develop her work. From the sentences collected from the online Bibliomancy Oracle, Lucila Mayol will map them randomly over the space of the exhibition.

Moiré VIII
Søren Krag

Moiré is a pattern or optical effect that occurs when two smooth, fine patterns are laid on top of each other and interfere, that is, together to form a third pattern.
In his new work, Søren will present how his digital Moiré has evolved.

Outdoors
Fleur Delaville & Lola Jacrot

During the period of confinement, Lola and Fleur recreated each other personal spaces with the video game medium. They explored the narration of our own lives, stopped in their courses.

Fleur and Lola explored each other cities on Google maps, searching for liminal spaces - locations that are a transition between two other locations, or states of being. Often empty and uncanny, they hold the potentiality of transgression and self-state transformations.

Memories of our fires, or global pyromancy attempt
Sandy Kalaydjian

Augurs, bad or good omens, sibylline words, futures glimpsed in ritualized hazards, Influencing and shaping the future that they’re looking for.
Making a film is choosing into an infinity of possibilities, then the story is told. Could a story be told in infinitely different ways, and be the same anyway?
Sandy will expand in three dimensions and loop her work over the space of the gallery.
